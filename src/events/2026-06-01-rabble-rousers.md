The documentary about how a working mother named Frances Goldin and her neighbours fought Robert Moses, the real estate industry, and five mayors — and built New York's first community land trust. It took them fifty years. They won.

I organized this screening to put an alternative on the table: that there are other ways to finance housing and other ways to hold land, and that we have already proved it works, in this city, in living memory. Then we sat down and argued about how to do it again.

**On the panel:** Tito Delgado, Cooper Square organizer and one of the people in the film. James DeFilippis, Rutgers professor and a founding member of NYCCLI. Council Member Alexa Avilés. And others. It was a wonderful conversation, and everybody left it thinking harder.
